Grenada SWOT Analysis

Stabalizing Destabalizing
Internal
  • Grenada is 96% Christian
  • 82% African
  • Grenada is divided into 6 different regions
  • Grenada has a literacy rate of 98.6%
  • Grenada has a federal government
  • Grenada is suffering from internal debt
External
  • Grenada participates in both the Carribean Community (CARICOM) and the
    Orginization of Eastern Carribean States (OCES)
  • Grenada has transfered between several different countries
  • Grenada is ruled by King Charles III
  • Grenada heavilly relies on tourism for income
  • Grenada has no military

Overall, Grenada is mostly a stable state, since it has a very similar ethnic population, and since most of the people are christian. This means that most of the people living in Grenada have the same religion and background, and so there is a lot more unity then somewhere with varying religions and backgrounds. Grenada also has a strong government, with a multi level system. Even though Grenada has transferred between several different powers, and is heavially reliant on tourism for income, I would still say that it is overall a pretty stable state
