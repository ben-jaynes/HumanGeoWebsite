Toponyms

Greece

  • Mykonos: named after it's first ruler, Mykons, who was believed to be the son or grandson of Apollo
  • Athens: Got it's name from the god Athena
  • Meteora: Derived from the Greek word meteoros meaning "raised from the ground"
  • Mount Athos: Athos is a giant who challenged the greek gods during the Gigantomachia
  • Rhodes: derived from the phoenician for snake, erod, since it was home to many snakes
  • Crete: might have been derived from one Kouretes who was named Kritas
  • Delphi: got it's name from the word dolphin, since it is said that Apollo got on a dolphin and rode from Crete to Delphi
  • Peloponnese: derived from a Greek myth which says that the hero Pelops conquered the whole region
  • Zagori: means "place behind the mountain"
  • Zakynthos: named after Zakynthos, son of a legendary Arcadian chief

Grenada

  • Grenada: likely named by Spanish sailors after the gity of Granada
  • St. Georges: named after the patron saint of England
  • Mt. Moritz: named after a french man, Harding Moritz
  • Gouave: named "L'Anse Goyave" or "Guava Cove" by it's original French inhabitants since guava trees grew so well
  • Eli Hill: name meaning "great hill" in French
  • Grenville: named after a British prime minister, George Grenville
  • Bogles: town named for the plantation of Robert Bogle
  • Carriacou: name derived from "Kayryouacou" meaning "land surrounded by reefs"
  • Mt. Parnassus: derived from the word parna meaning house
  • Grand Roy: the name means great king
